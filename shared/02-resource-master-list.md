# Cybersecurity Operations – Resources Master List

This file collects the main learning platforms, roadmaps, and reference sites mentioned across all cohorts. Use it to customise your 90‑day plan.

---

## 1. Core Learning Platforms

### Hands‑on labs (all levels)

- **TryHackMe** – Guided, browser‑based labs with beginner and blue‑team paths.
- **Hack The Box / HTB Academy** – More advanced, challenge‑based labs and blue‑team content.
- **Blue Team Labs Online (BTLO)** – Blue‑team/SOC‑focused challenges.
- **RangeForce / HackTheBox Blue Teams / OffSec Blue Team** – Enterprise‑grade blue‑team/SOC training ranges.

### Video and course libraries

- **Professor Messer (Security+)** – Free Security+ video series.
- **NetworkChuck / David Bombal** – Networking, Linux, and security fundamentals content.
- **Cybrary** – Large course library and cert prep across security topics.
- **Coursera / edX** – University and vendor courses (intro cyber, cloud security).

### Vendor fundamentals

- **Cisco Networking Academy – Introduction to Cybersecurity.**
- **IBM – Cybersecurity Fundamentals.**
- **EC‑Council – free beginner cybersecurity course options.**
- **Microsoft Learn – Security Operations Analyst / SC‑200 learning path.**
- **AWS / Azure / Google Cloud security fundamentals training.**

---

## 2. Blue Team / SOC / DFIR Resources

### Roadmaps and guides

- **Blue Team Training Roadmap (various blog/roadmap PDFs).**
- **“Blue Team Training Roadmap” – Just Hacking.**
- **“How to Become a SOC Analyst” – SANS Institute.**
- **SOC Analyst career‑path guides (Cybrary, Dropzone, vendor blogs).**
- **DFIR career and learning path articles (DFIR Diva, EC‑Council, others).**

### Tools and SIEM practice

- **Splunk Fundamentals 1** – Free SIEM fundamentals course.
- **Elastic / ELK Stack tutorials** – Log collection, search, and dashboards.
- **Microsoft Sentinel learning paths** – Cloud‑native SIEM practice.

### DFIR practice

- **LetsDefend DFIR and SOC learning paths.**
- **Blue‑team ranges (HTB, RangeForce, etc.) with IR/DFIR scenarios.**

---

## 3. Cloud & Security Engineering

- **AWS / Azure / GCP official security docs and training tracks.**
- **Cloud provider “security fundamentals” or “architect associate” level courses.**
- **Hands‑on cloud labs using free tiers (build, misconfigure, then harden).**
- **IaC and automation tutorials (Terraform, CloudFormation, Bicep, etc.) for security guardrails.**

Use these mainly for Cohort 1 and Cohort 3 learners targeting **cloud security** or **security engineering** roles.

---

## 4. GRC, Risk & Compliance

- **High‑level overviews of frameworks:** ISO 27001, NIST CSF, SOC 2 summaries.
- **GRC career‑path articles** (e.g., Sprinto, ComplyJet, Cyber Management Alliance).
- **Basic risk management tutorials** – likelihood/impact, controls, registers.
- **Public breach reports / regulatory reports** – for practice writing short analyses.

These are especially relevant for **Cohort 2** (career changers) and **Cohort 3** PM/BA/ITSM backgrounds.

---

## 5. Career Change & Transition Guides

### For career changers

- Articles on **“how to switch careers to cybersecurity”** from major education providers.
- Blogs and guides on mapping **non‑IT backgrounds** to cyber roles.
- Career‑switch specific paths (SOC, GRC, Cloud) from platforms like LetsDefend.

### For existing IT professionals

- Guides on transitioning from **IT support, sysadmin, network, dev, DevOps** into security.
- Internal‑transition playbooks (add security to your current job, then pivot).

These support the 30‑60‑90 plans for **Cohort 2** and **Cohort 3**.

## 7. Quick Picks by Cohort

### Cohort 1 – Students & Grads

- TryHackMe or HTB Academy beginner and blue‑team paths.
- Blue Team Labs / BTLO for SOC‑style challenges.
- Professor Messer / Security+ playlists for theory.
- Local BSides / OWASP events; student security clubs.

### Cohort 2 – Career Changers

- One structured intro course (e.g., “Intro to Cybersecurity”) plus:
  - SOC/blue‑team labs (TryHackMe, LetsDefend, BTLO), or
  - GRC resources (framework summaries, risk templates), or
  - Cloud security fundamentals (AWS/Azure/GCP).
- Career‑change articles and mentorship platforms for guidance.

### Cohort 3 – IT Professionals

- Platform‑specific security training (e.g., Microsoft / AWS / Azure security paths).
- SIEM/logging practice (Splunk / Sentinel / Elastic).
- AppSec/DevSecOps content (for dev/DevOps).
- GRC / program‑management resources (for PM/BA/ITSM roles).

---

## How to Use This File

- Pick **1–2 platforms** as your main lab environment, not everything at once.
- Match resources to your **cohort** and **target path** (SOC, GRC, Cloud, AppSec, DFIR).
- Plug them into your 30‑60‑90 plan as specific weekly actions (e.g., “Finish TryHackMe module X”, “Draft risk register for lab environment”).

Treat this list as a menu, not a checklist – your 90‑day plan should be opinionated and realistic, not an attempt to do everything.
