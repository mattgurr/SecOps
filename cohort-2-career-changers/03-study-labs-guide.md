# Cohort 2 - 03 - 90-Day Study & Lab Guide – Career Changers

Assume ~5–8 hours/week, built to be realistic around life and work.

## Days 1–30 – Foundations & Mapping

### Study (3–4 hrs/week)
- IT foundations refresh: networking, OS basics, what a SOC does.
- Overview of key roles (SOC, IR, GRC, cloud security, DFIR).
- One structured intro path (e.g., “Intro to Cybersecurity” MOOC or roadmap).

Focus:
- Map your existing skills (analysis, writing, process, customer work) against roles.
- Decide: blue‑team/SOC vs GRC vs cloud as your primary direction.

### Labs (2–3 hrs/week)
- Do 4–6 beginner labs that match your chosen direction:
  - Blue team: beginner SOC/defensive labs.
  - GRC: basic risk assessment exercises (even spreadsheet‑based).
  - Cloud: secure vs insecure sample deployments using tutorials.
- Build your first very small “artifact”:
  - One-page incident summary, risk register, or system hardening checklist—whatever fits your direction.

Checklist by day 30:
- [ ] Written mapping of your previous career skills to security tasks.
- [ ] At least 4 labs completed.
- [ ] One tiny artifact that shows your thinking in your target path.

## Days 31–60 – Role-Aligned Skills & Artifacts

Split into suggested tracks; you can mix, but pick a primary.

### Track A – SOC / IR (Blue Team)

Study (3–4 hrs/week):
- Logging, SIEM, alert triage concepts.
- Basic IR process and terminology.

Labs (3–4 hrs/week):
- Use SOC‑focused ranges / Academy modules that:
  - Walk through alerts and triage process.
- In your own or a cloud‑hosted lab:
  - Simulate at least one brute‑force or malware‑like behaviour.
  - Practice writing an alert narrative (“what happened, why it matters, what we did”).

Outputs:
- Short “triage notes” for 2–3 sample alerts.

### Track B – GRC / Policy

Study (3–4 hrs/week):
- High‑level view of security frameworks (e.g., ISO 27001, NIST CSF) via summaries.
- Basic risk concepts: likelihood, impact, controls.

Labs (3–4 hrs/week):
- Create a simple risk register for:
  - A small business or a system you understand.
- Draft one short policy or standard (e.g., “Acceptable Use”, “Password Policy”).
- Review a public breach report and write a half‑page “what went wrong” analysis.

Outputs:
- A small GRC pack: one risk register + one short policy + one breach analysis.

### Track C – Cloud Security

Study (3–4 hrs/week):
- Cloud security 101 (shared responsibility, IAM basics, logging).
- Provider‑specific security best practices (at a high level).

Labs (3–4 hrs/week):
- Use a free tier account:
  - Deploy a basic service (VM or managed service).
  - Turn on security logging.
- Deliberately misconfigure one thing (overly broad access) and then fix it.
- Capture before/after and the logs that changed.

Outputs:
- A short writeup with screenshots of misconfig → fix → logs.

Checklist by day 60:
- [ ] 1–2 artifacts aligned with your chosen role.
- [ ] Confidence explaining basic concepts in your direction.
- [ ] Notes on what you enjoy vs what feels like a chore.

## Days 61–90 – Portfolio & Interview Readiness

### Study (2–3 hrs/week)
- Focus on gaps found in labs/practice tests.
- Review at least 20–30 common questions for your target roles (tech + behavioural).

### Labs (3–5 hrs/week)
Goal: “Interviewable” stories.

- Extend one earlier project into something portfolio‑ready:
  - Blue team: a small series of triage case studies from SOC labs.
  - GRC: mini “security program” for a small fictional company.
  - Cloud: mini “security review” of a demo environment with issues and fixes.
- Practice explaining:
  - What the environment was.
  - What problem you tackled.
  - What you did, what you learned, what you’d do next.

Checklist by day 90:
- [ ] 2–3 concise project/lab writeups you can walk through in interviews.
- [ ] A study list for the next 3–6 months (cert, deeper path).
- [ ] Clear notes on which roles you’re actually targeting now.
